Jennifer C. Ast, PhD

Clear Text offers text editing and writing coaching for academic texts.

Employment

From 2015 until now, I've been running Ast Clear Text, offering services from coaching to proofreading for scholarly texts in English.

Between 2013 and 2016, I was a language tutor at Uppsala University’s Language Workshop (Språkverkstaden).

Education

From 2008 to 2010, I was involved in a post-doctoral project at Uppsala, using metagenomics and next-generation DNA sequencing to investigate bacterial diversity in Swedish lakes.

Before that, I was a research fellow at the University of Michigan (2002-2007); my research used DNA to study the ecology and evolutionary relationships of luminous marine bacteria symbiotic with fishes and squids. I also received my Masters and Ph.D. from Michigan, where my research was about the evolutionary relationships among turtles, certain types of lizards, and between lizards and snakes.

I received my B.S. from the University of Chicago, where I was a biology major, honor student, and soccer player.

Here's what I bring to the table

Years of practical writing experience

I have myself written and published peer-reviewed publications, and I understand that expressing abstract ideas can be very difficult.

Years of relevant work experience

I have myself been a university researcher, and I know firsthand the stress of writing a thesis, and the pressures to get grants and publications.

A convenient location

I live in Uppsala, and am happy to meet face-to-face to discuss your text.

I like reading research and helping people clarify their ideas.

I have always enjoyed reading about a variety of topics and subjects. When I read new research, I try to understand it. I will find your text interesting, and I will enjoy helping you get it out into the world.

I appreciate diversity.

I have worked with people from many different countries with many different first languages. Having tried to do it myself, I am especially sympathetic to the difficulties of writing academic texts in a foreign language.

I understand academia and its ways.

I speak geek.
